Dispatch Time

With the following data visuals, it is easy to visualize relative dispatch times amongst the different areas in San Francisco. For the sake of clarity, these visuals are not representative of all outliers in dispatch times across locations.

Excluding all outliers as determined by the displayed boxplots, the 43, 33, and 8 station areas have the longest dispatch times.


Again, excluding outliers, 94127, 94111, 94134, and 94105, take the longest to dispatch to. These dispatch times can be reduced by reassigning stations of low dispatch times and frequencies to the zipcodes that have greater dispatch times and high call frequencies.

Increase in Dispatch Calls

The number of dispatch calls in San Francisco has shown to have increased rapidly during the population surge. It is imperative for the city's emergency units to prepare for the increased demand of their services. Understanding the precise locations that will be affected most by the change in the city's population will allow units to allocate more resources in such areas of need. The following visuals manifest the areas that require urgent attention as their dispatch call frequencies have doubled. This data considers the range of call frequencies rather than the increase relative to the average number of calls. Relating this data to the average number of calls expected prior to the population surge would be most beneficial; however, call data prior to the population surge is currently unavailable.

The zip codes that must be attended to first are 94127 and 94104 - their call frequencies are over ten times the norm.



Since the represented box areas of high increase in dispatch calls are of similar rates of increase, the units should tend to these box areas by their relative zip codes.










These charts display the most common dispatch unit types in each zip code/box. Relative to their locations, these are the unit-types that should be prioritized during expansion.
